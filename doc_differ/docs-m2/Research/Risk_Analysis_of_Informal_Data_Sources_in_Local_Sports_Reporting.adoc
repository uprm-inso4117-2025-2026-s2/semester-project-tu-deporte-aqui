= Handling Informal Sports Data Risks in Live Systems
Project: Tu Deporte Aquí

---

== 1. Conflicting Real-Time Reports in Fast-Moving Digital Platforms

Research shows that social media platforms prioritize speed over verification. During live events, early reports are frequently corrected later, and multiple independent accounts may report different values before confirmation occurs.

In live BSN games, it is common to observe different scores posted at nearly the same time by league Facebook pages, team Instagram accounts, and fan accounts.

Because updates are manually entered and unsynchronized, discrepancies may occur within seconds.

=== How Informal Reporting Creates Conflicts

* *Simultaneous Independent Posts:* Different reporters publish scores based on observation
* *Manual Entry Errors:* Typographical mistakes or misread plays
* *Correction After Publication:* Updates may be edited without visibility of the original error

This demonstrates that systems depending on informal sources must expect temporary inconsistencies.

Sources:  
https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.31.2.211

== 2. Latency and Irregular Update Cycles

Digital news research indicates that human-managed accounts update irregularly.

Unlike structured APIs, social media platforms:

* Do not guarantee update intervals
* May pause reporting due to staffing or connectivity

In Liga de Béisbol Superior Doble A, updates may have multi-minute gaps while the game continues.

=== How Informal Reporting Creates Latency Issues

* *Non-Standard Update Frequency*
* *Human Dependency*
* *Connectivity Constraints*

This shows that systems must treat missing updates as potential delay, not final state.

Sources:  
https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/digital-news-report

== 3. Incomplete or Context-Less Game Information

User-generated content often lacks structured metadata such as:

* Game clock
* Inning or set
* Final confirmation

In Voleibol Superior matches, posts may show scores without indicating match status.

=== How Informal Reporting Creates Ambiguity

* Missing period identifiers
* No timestamp included
* No explicit game status (Live vs Final)

Systems must avoid inferring missing information.

---

== 4. Premature or Incorrect Final Status Declarations

Real-time systems research shows early conclusions are often revised.

During BSN playoff games:

* Accounts may post *Final* immediately after the buzzer
* Later corrections (e.g., overtime) invalidate those posts

=== How Informal Reporting Leads to Status Errors

* Immediate publishing after apparent end
* Lack of official verification
* Post-event corrections

Systems risk marking games as final prematurely.

Sources:  
https://doi.org/10.1145/3097286.3097341

== 5. Content Volatility Through Edits and Deletions

Social media allows edits and deletions without transparent history.

Local sports pages often:

* Edit captions to correct scores
* Delete incorrect posts

=== How Informal Platforms Create Integrity Risks

* Editable captions
* Deleted posts
* No version tracking

This creates traceability risks for automated systems.

Sources:  
https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.aap9559

== 6. Unverified Community-Driven Claims During Live Events

Community-driven content spreads rapidly without verification.

During live games:

* Fan pages may report injuries or score changes early
* Information may later be corrected or ignored

=== How Informal Reporting Amplifies Unverified Claims

* Rapid sharing without validation
* High engagement incentives
* Emotion-driven posting

Systems must include verification safeguards.

Sources:  
https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1806781116

== 7. Engineering Implications for Live Game Systems

Industry best practices emphasize:

* Validation
* Timestamp tracking
* Multi-source reconciliation

Sources:  
https://martinfowler.com/articles/patterns-of-distributed-systems/  
https://12factor.net/

=== How Engineering Standards Address Informal Data Risks

* *Multi-Source Verification:* Require agreement before confirming final states
* *Timestamp Transparency:* Display last updated time
* *Conflict Detection:* Identify inconsistent values
* *Data Integrity Controls:* Avoid silent overwriting
* *Explicit Unknown States:* Do not infer missing data

---

== 8. Recommended Strategies for Handling Informal Data Risks

=== 1. Require Multi-Source Confirmation for Final Status

Do not mark a game as final unless confirmed by:

* An official source, or
* At least two independent sources

---

=== 2. Display Last Updated Timestamp

Show when the score was last refreshed to reduce perceived latency.

---

=== 3. Implement Data Confidence Levels

Indicate:

* High
* Medium
* Low

Based on source reliability and agreement.

---

=== 4. Flag Conflicting Scores

If discrepancies are detected:

* Show a warning
* Avoid silently selecting one value

---

=== 5. Avoid Inferring Missing Game State

If inning, quarter, or set is missing:

* Mark as *unknown*
* Do not guess

---

=== 6. Rank Sources by Reliability

Prioritize:

* Official league sources
* Verified partners
* Reporters
* Community sources

---

== 9. Final Definition of Live Game Status Reliability

A live game status is considered reliable only when:

* The score has a visible timestamp
* Source reliability is documented
* No unresolved conflicts exist
* Final status is confirmed via multi-source validation

Any data not meeting these conditions should be treated as *provisional*.
