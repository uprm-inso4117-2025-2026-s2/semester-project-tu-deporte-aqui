==== 1.2.1

===== Current Situation

This section describes how sports information about local Puerto Rico events is currently produced, shared, and consulted, without assuming any particular system.

Local sports information is not provided through a single coordinated source. Instead, it appears across multiple channels. In practice, people rely on a combination of league websites, team pages, social media posts, sports media, third-party platforms, and informal community reporting to follow schedules, scores, standings, and event updates.

This description is based on project research into how local leagues publish updates, how people discover events, and how standings, scores, and live data are communicated.

====== Information Sources in the Current Environment

Sports information appears through several types of sources:

* official league websites
* team and league social media pages
* sports media outlets
* independent reporters and community pages
* third-party sports platforms

These sources differ in format, timing, and level of detail. Some provide structured data, while others provide short updates intended for immediate awareness.

====== Fragmentation of Information

Information about the same event is often split across multiple sources.

For example:

* a league page may publish an official result after a reporter at the venue
* a team account may post a score without additional context
* a media outlet may summarize standings without showing the full table

To understand a single event, a person may need to consult several sources.

====== Irregular Timing and Delay

Updates are not synchronized across sources.

Observed patterns include:

* manual posting of scores
* gaps between updates during active games
* delayed publication of final results or standings
* sources reflecting different points in time

Two sources checked at the same moment may show different versions of the same event.

====== Inconsistency and Incompleteness

Information may differ in both content and completeness.

Examples include:

* different scores reported for the same game
* inconsistent standings or rankings
* missing time indicators or status labels
* updates that do not indicate whether they are live or final

These differences often result from manual entry, different publication practices, and lack of coordination between sources.

====== Consequences for People in the Domain

The current situation affects different roles:

* fans and families may have difficulty deciding which information to trust
* players and coaches may need to verify schedules and results across sources
* leagues and organizers may see outdated or partial information circulate
* reporters and community contributors often publish quickly without full confirmation

Following local sports often requires comparing multiple sources rather than relying on a single consistent view.
