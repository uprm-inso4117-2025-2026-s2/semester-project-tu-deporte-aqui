== [.inserted]#Objective#

[.inserted]#The objective of this research is to design a transparency feature that explains to users why a specific news item appears in their feed.#

== [.inserted]#Problem#

[.inserted]#When users do not understand how their feed behaves, confusion increases.#

[.inserted]#A short “reason label” per story can:#

* [.inserted]#Reduce confusion#
* [.inserted]#Improve trust#
* [.inserted]#Allow users to decide whether they want to continue seeing similar content#

== [.inserted]#UX Principles#

* [.inserted]#*Non-intrusive*: Must be short and direct#
* [.inserted]#*Privacy*: Do not expose sensitive information#
* [.inserted]#*Actionable*: Must include options such as mute, follow, or manage preferences#

== [.inserted]#UI Placement Options#

=== [.inserted]#Option A (Recommended): Micro-label under headline#

[.inserted]#Place a one-line reason label under the story title, before time/source metadata.#

==== [.inserted]#Pros#

* [.inserted]#Always visible#
* [.inserted]#Builds trust passively#

==== [.inserted]#Cons#

* [.inserted]#Must remain short (one line maximum)#

=== [.inserted]#Option B: Info icon only#

[.inserted]#Show only an `(i)` icon near time/source; tapping reveals the reason.#

==== [.inserted]#Pros#

* [.inserted]#Minimal visual clutter#

==== [.inserted]#Cons#

* [.inserted]#Lower discoverability#
