== [.inserted]#Research Summary#

[.inserted]#This research examines how different sports apps implement Favorites systems and how those selections influence the user’s personalized home or feed experience.#

== [.inserted]#1. How Favorites Are Added and Managed#

[.inserted]#Across platforms, three common entry points were identified:#

=== [.inserted]#Onboarding Selection#

[.inserted]#During first launch, users are prompted to select favorite teams, leagues, or athletes.  #
[.inserted]#This allows the app to personalize content immediately.#

=== [.inserted]#Search-Based Favoriting#

[.inserted]#Users can search for a team or league and tap actions such as *Follow* or *Favorite*.  #
[.inserted]#This supports gradual personalization over time.#

=== [.inserted]#Contextual Favoriting#

[.inserted]#Users can favorite directly from match pages, team profiles, or standings tables without leaving the current view.#

---

=== [.inserted]#Management Patterns#

[.inserted]#Favorites are typically managed through:#

* [.inserted]#A dedicated Favorites section#
* [.inserted]#Profile or settings pages#
* [.inserted]#Editable lists (add / remove)#

[.inserted]#Most apps allow multiple favorites but prioritize certain entities in the interface.#

== [.inserted]#2. Where Favorites Appear in the App#

[.inserted]#Favorites influence several interface areas:#

=== [.inserted]#Navigation Access#

[.inserted]#Some platforms include a Favorites or *My Teams* tab for quick access.#

=== [.inserted]#Home Feed Prioritization#

[.inserted]#Matches, leagues, and news related to favorites appear first in the feed.#

=== [.inserted]#Notifications#

[.inserted]#Favorites often drive alerts such as match start times or score updates.#

== [.inserted]#3. Personalized Feed Content Patterns#

[.inserted]#Common modules powered by favorites include:#

=== [.inserted]#Live and Recent Scores#

[.inserted]#Ongoing or recently completed matches involving followed entities.#

=== [.inserted]#Upcoming Fixtures#

[.inserted]#Next scheduled matches with date and time indicators.#

=== [.inserted]#Standings#

[.inserted]#League tables and rankings connected to favorite teams.#

=== [.inserted]#News and Updates#

[.inserted]#Injuries, transfers, or match highlights, depending on platform focus.#

---

== [.inserted]#4. Behavior When No Favorites Exist#

[.inserted]#All analyzed apps implement fallback experiences.#

=== [.inserted]#Prompt-Based Empty State#

[.inserted]#Users are encouraged to select favorites to improve personalization.#

=== [.inserted]#Generic Feed Fallback#

[.inserted]#Until favorites are added, the app displays:#

* [.inserted]#Featured matches#
* [.inserted]#Popular leagues#
* [.inserted]#National events#

[.inserted]#This ensures usability even without user preference data.#

== [.inserted]#5. Extracted Design Patterns#

[.inserted]#Several repeatable system patterns emerged:#

=== [.inserted]#Follow Graph Model#

[.inserted]#Users establish relationships with teams, leagues, or athletes that drive personalization.#

=== [.inserted]#Priority Sorting#

[.inserted]#Favorite entities rank higher than general or trending content.#

=== [.inserted]#Modular Feed Structure#

[.inserted]#Feeds are composed of blocks (scores, standings, news) filtered by favorites.#

=== [.inserted]#Rule-Based Personalization#

[.inserted]#Most systems rely on simple logic rather than machine learning.#
