= [.inserted]#Event Discovery & Browsing Patterns in Sports Platforms#
[.inserted]#Project: Tu Deporte Aquí#

== [.inserted]#Research Summary#

[.inserted]#Focus: Event discovery, browsing, and information organization#

---

== [.inserted]#Purpose#

[.inserted]#This research was conducted to understand how existing sports information platforms structure and present event listings in a way that supports easy browsing and discovery.#

[.inserted]#The goal is to identify common organizational patterns that help users quickly find relevant sports events without relying on complex search or prior context.#

---

== [.inserted]#Platforms Analyzed#

[.inserted]#The following platforms were reviewed due to their relevance to sports event discovery and scheduling:#

* [.inserted]#*ESPN* – Broad sports coverage with structured league and event timelines#
* [.inserted]#*Flashscore* – Live-score platform emphasizing chronological event browsing#
* [.inserted]#*BSNF* – Regional event listings (Baloncesto Superior Nacional Femenino)#

---

== [.inserted]#Common Event Discovery Patterns Identified#

[.inserted]#Across the analyzed platforms, several consistent patterns emerged:#

* [.inserted]#Chronological grouping of events (today, upcoming, completed)#
* [.inserted]#Clear visual separation between scheduled, live, and finished events#
* [.inserted]#League or sport-based categorization as a primary navigation method#
* [.inserted]#Simple filtering mechanisms rather than advanced search#
* [.inserted]#Consistent event cards showing essential information at a glance#

---

== [.inserted]#Usability Observations#

* [.inserted]#Users tend to browse events chronologically rather than search by name#
* [.inserted]#Separating upcoming and completed events reduces confusion#
* [.inserted]#Simple layouts support casual sports fans better than dense dashboards#
* [.inserted]#Overly detailed views distract from discovery and exploration#

---

== [.inserted]#Key Takeaways for Project#

[.inserted]#Based on these findings:#

* [.inserted]#Chronological browsing should be the default discovery method#
* [.inserted]#Event status should be clearly represented through structure, not messaging#
* [.inserted]#League and sport grouping should guide navigation decisions#
* [.inserted]#Event listings should remain lightweight and scannable#
* [.inserted]#The platform should resemble a local sports bulletin rather than a statistics-heavy system#

[.inserted]#These insights will guide navigation, structure, and content organization decisions in future milestones.#
