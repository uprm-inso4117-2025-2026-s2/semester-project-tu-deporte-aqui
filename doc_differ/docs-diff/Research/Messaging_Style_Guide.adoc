= Tu Deporte Aquí — Messaging Style Guide

== Objective

Create a clear, consistent *tone* and *language style* guide for *Tu Deporte Aquí* updates (Spanish-first + optional English), so messages remain calm, readable, and trustworthy.

---

== 1) Research Local PR Sports Writing Norms

=== A) Official League/Team Social Posts (Instagram)

* *Tone:* Professional, trustworthy, no sensationalism.

*Key Terms:*
* *OFICIAL:* for confirmed, reliable updates (postponements, final scores, etc.)
* *EN DESARROLLO:* used for ongoing updates
* *ACTUALIZADO:* indicates updated status (e.g., corrections)

*Common Structure:*
* "OFICIAL: [Event] ha sido [confirmed action], próximo paso: [next action]."
* "ACTUALIZADO: [Score] ahora es [new value]."

---

=== B) Puerto Rico Sports Media (El Nuevo Día / Primera Hora)

* *Tone:* Informative and concrete; avoid speculation.

*Key Terms:*
* *Última Hora:* breaking news
* *Marcador Final:* final score announcement
* *Confirmado:* verified information

*Common Structure:*
* "Última Hora: [Event] se ha [confirmed action]."
* "Marcador Final: [Team A] [score] – [Team B] [score]."

---

=== C) TV/Social Sports (WAPA Deportes on Facebook)

* *Tone:* Short and direct

*Key Terms:*
* *FINAL:* finalized scores
* *CONFIRMADO:* verified events
* *En Vivo:* live updates

*Common Structure:*
* "FINAL: [Team A] [score] – [Team B] [score]."
* "CONFIRMADO: [Event] [action taken]."

---

== 2) Define Platform Conventions

=== Spanish-First Terminology

* *OFICIAL:* confirmed information
* *En Desarrollo:* evolving information
* *Confirmado:* verified and final
* *Actualizado:* corrected or updated information

---

=== Do’s and Don’ts for Tone

*Do:*
* Use clear, factual language
* Maintain a neutral, calm tone
* Label uncertain or developing information clearly

*Don’t:*
* Use sensational language or clickbait
* Speculate before verification
* Omit labels when information is unclear

---

=== Consistent Formatting

*Caps Usage:*
* Use *ALL CAPS* for key updates like *OFICIAL* or *FINAL*
* Avoid ALL CAPS for full sentences

*Punctuation:*
* Use *em dashes* for transitions  
  Example: "Confirmado: El evento se ha reprogramado – próximo partido será el [fecha]"
* Avoid excessive exclamation marks or dramatic elements

*Emojis:*
* 🚨 for urgent updates
* ✅ for confirmed updates

---

== 3) Approved Phrasing Library

=== Common Update Types

==== 1) Final Score Update

*Spanish:*  
"Final: [Equipo A] [3]–[2] [Equipo B]. Gracias por seguirnos."

*English:*  
"Final: [Team A] [3]–[2] [Team B]. Thanks for following."

---

==== 2) Postponement Announcement

*Spanish:*  
"*OFICIAL*: El partido de [Equipo A] vs [Equipo B] ha sido *pospuesto* debido a [razón]. Nueva fecha será anunciada pronto."

*English:*  
"*OFFICIAL*: The match between [Team A] vs [Team B] has been *postponed* due to [reason]. New date will be announced soon."

---

==== 3) Developing / Unverified Information

*Spanish:*  
"*En desarrollo*: Se están reportando rumores de [evento], pero *no confirmado* por fuentes oficiales. Última actualización: [hora]."

*English:*  
"*Developing*: Reports indicate [event], but *not confirmed* by official sources. Last update: [time]."

---

==== 4) Correction / Updated Information

*Spanish:*  
"Corrección: El marcador que publicamos anteriormente era incorrecto. *Confirmado ahora*: [marcador correcto]."

*English:*  
"Correction: The score we posted earlier was incorrect. *Confirmed now*: [correct score]."

---

== 4) Testing Plan

=== Paper Scenarios to Validate

* *Postponement announcement:* Ensure clarity and professionalism
* *Correction to a previously posted result:* Ensure transparency
* *Delayed update message:* Ensure neutral tone and clear expectations
