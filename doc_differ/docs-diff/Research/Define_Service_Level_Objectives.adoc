== [.inserted]#2. Proposed Service-Level Objectives (SLOs)#

=== [.inserted]#SLO 1 – Live Score Update Latency#

Metric::

[.inserted]#Time between official score update and display on the platform#

Target::

[.inserted]#≤ 30 seconds during live events#

Explanation::

[.inserted]#Real-time systems may aim for sub-second updates, but this platform is not in a betting environment.#

[.inserted]#A 30-second delay:#

* [.inserted]#Maintains perceived "live" status#
* [.inserted]#Avoids unnecessary infrastructure complexity#

Why this matters::

[.inserted]#If scores lag significantly, users may:#

* [.inserted]#Compare with social media#
* [.inserted]#Lose trust in the platform#

Assumption::

[.inserted]#During peak load, delays up to 60 seconds may be acceptable if clearly communicated#

---

=== [.inserted]#SLO 2 – Mean Time to Recovery (MTTR)#

Metric::

[.inserted]#Time required to restore system after partial failure#

Target::

[.inserted]#≤ 10 minutes#

Explanation::

[.inserted]#Fast recovery is often more important than avoiding all failures.#

[.inserted]#A system that:#

* [.inserted]#fails briefly#
* [.inserted]#recovers quickly#

[.inserted]#is perceived as more reliable than one with long outages#

Why this matters::

[.inserted]#Sports events are time-sensitive.#

Example::

[.inserted]#A 30-minute outage during a final would significantly damage user trust#

Assumption::

[.inserted]#Recovery includes restoring data feeds, not necessarily replaying missed updates#
