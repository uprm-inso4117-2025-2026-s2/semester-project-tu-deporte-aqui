== [.inserted]#1. Conflicting Real-Time Reports in Fast-Moving Digital Platforms#

[.inserted]#Research shows that social media platforms prioritize speed over verification. During live events, early reports are frequently corrected later, and multiple independent accounts may report different values before confirmation occurs.#

[.inserted]#In live BSN games, it is common to observe different scores posted at nearly the same time by league Facebook pages, team Instagram accounts, and fan accounts.#

[.inserted]#Because updates are manually entered and unsynchronized, discrepancies may occur within seconds.#

=== [.inserted]#How Informal Reporting Creates Conflicts#

* [.inserted]#*Simultaneous Independent Posts:* Different reporters publish scores based on observation#
* [.inserted]#*Manual Entry Errors:* Typographical mistakes or misread plays#
* [.inserted]#*Correction After Publication:* Updates may be edited without visibility of the original error#

[.inserted]#This demonstrates that systems depending on informal sources must expect temporary inconsistencies.#

[.inserted]#Sources:  #
[.inserted]#https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.31.2.211#
