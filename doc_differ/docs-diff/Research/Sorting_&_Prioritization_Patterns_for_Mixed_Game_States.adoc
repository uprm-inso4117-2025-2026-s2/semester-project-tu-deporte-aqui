[.inserted]#. Scheduled Games#

* [.inserted]#Scheduled games appear last.#
* [.inserted]#The structure prioritizes recent relevance (what just happened) over upcoming events.#

=== [.inserted]#NBA App#

[.inserted]#Ordering Pattern:#

[.inserted]#. Live Games#
[.inserted]#. Scheduled Games#
[.inserted]#. Final Scores#

[.inserted]#Observations:#

* [.inserted]#Live games are consistently prioritized.#
* [.inserted]#Upcoming games follow in chronological order.#
* [.inserted]#Final games are placed at the bottom.#
* [.inserted]#Emphasis is placed on current and future events.#

[.inserted]#NBA follows a forward-looking ordering model.#

== [.inserted]#Key Patterns Identified#

[.inserted]#All applications prioritize Live games at the top of the list.#

[.inserted]#However, they differ in how they prioritize:#

* [.inserted]#What has just finished (Final)#
* [.inserted]#What is upcoming (Scheduled)#

[.inserted]#Two dominant models emerge:#

=== [.inserted]#1. Recency-Oriented (ESPN)#

[.inserted]#Live → Final → Scheduled#

[.inserted]#Focuses on what is happening now and what just happened.#

=== [.inserted]#2. Forward-Looking (NBA & Real)#

[.inserted]#Live → Scheduled → Final#

[.inserted]#Focuses on what is happening now and what comes next.#

== [.inserted]#Recommendation for this Project#

[.inserted]#Adopt the forward-looking model:#

[.inserted]#. Live games#
[.inserted]#. Scheduled games (chronological order)#
[.inserted]#. Final games (most recent first)#

[.inserted]#Live → Scheduled → Final#

[.inserted]#Rationale:#

* [.inserted]#Aligns with the majority pattern (NBA and Real).#
* [.inserted]#Keeps user attention on current and upcoming events.#
* [.inserted]#Improves scan-ability and anticipation.#
* [.inserted]#Reduces cognitive load by separating active, future, and completed states clearly.#
