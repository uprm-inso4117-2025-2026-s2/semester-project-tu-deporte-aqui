= Sorting & Prioritization Patterns for Mixed Game States

== Key Structural Difference

=== ESPN

Ordering Pattern:

. Live Games
. Final Scores
. Scheduled Games

Observations:

* Live games always appear at the top.
* Recently completed games (Final) appear immediately after Live games.
* Scheduled games appear last.
* The structure prioritizes recent relevance (what just happened) over upcoming events.

ESPN follows a recency-oriented ordering model.

=== NBA App

Ordering Pattern:

. Live Games
. Scheduled Games
. Final Scores

Observations:

* Live games are consistently prioritized.
* Upcoming games follow in chronological order.
* Final games are placed at the bottom.
* Emphasis is placed on current and future events.

NBA follows a forward-looking ordering model.

=== Real App

Ordering Pattern:

. Live Games
. Scheduled Games
. Final Scores

Observations:

* Same structural pattern as NBA.
* Live games receive clear visual emphasis.
* Scheduled games appear before completed games.

Real also follows a forward-looking ordering model.

== Key Patterns Identified

All applications prioritize Live games at the top of the list.

However, they differ in how they prioritize:

* What has just finished (Final)
* What is upcoming (Scheduled)

Two dominant models emerge:

=== 1. Recency-Oriented (ESPN)

Live → Final → Scheduled

Focuses on what is happening now and what just happened.

=== 2. Forward-Looking (NBA & Real)

Live → Scheduled → Final

Focuses on what is happening now and what comes next.

== Recommendation for this Project

Adopt the forward-looking model:

. Live games
. Scheduled games (chronological order)
. Final games (most recent first)

Live → Scheduled → Final

Rationale:

* Aligns with the majority pattern (NBA and Real).
* Keeps user attention on current and upcoming events.
* Improves scan-ability and anticipation.
* Reduces cognitive load by separating active, future, and completed states clearly.
