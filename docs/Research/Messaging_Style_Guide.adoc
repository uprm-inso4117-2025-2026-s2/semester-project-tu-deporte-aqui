Tu Deporte Aquí — Messaging Style Guide
Objective
Create a clear, consistent tone and language style guide for Tu Deporte Aquí updates (Spanish-first + optional English), so messages remain calm, readable, and trustworthy.

1) Research Local PR Sports Writing Norms
A) Official League/Team Social Posts (Instagram)
Tone: Professional, trustworthy, no sensationalism.
Key Terms:
OFICIAL: for confirmed, reliable updates (postponements, final scores, etc.)
EN DESARROLLO: used for ongoing updates, particularly during evolving situations.
ACTUALIZADO: to indicate an updated status (e.g., score corrections, news updates).
Common Structure:
"OFICIAL: [Event] ha sido [confirmed action], próximo paso: [next action]."
"ACTUALIZADO: [Score] ahora es [new value]."
B) Puerto Rico Sports Media (El Nuevo Día / Primera Hora)
Tone: Informative and concrete; avoid speculation.
Key Terms:
Última Hora: reserved for breaking news (e.g., delays, injuries).
Marcador Final: used to announce the final score of a game.
Confirmado: used when a report is verified.
Common Structure:
"Última Hora: [Event] se ha [confirmed action]."
"Marcador Final: [Team A] [score] – [Team B] [score]."
C) TV/Social Sports (WAPA Deportes on Facebook)
Tone: Short and direct, optimized for social media.
Key Terms:
FINAL: used for finalized scores.
CONFIRMADO: indicates an event has been verified.
En Vivo: used for live updates or events happening right now.
Common Structure:
"FINAL: [Team A] [score] – [Team B] [score]."
"CONFIRMADO: [Event] [action taken]."
2) Define Platform Conventions
Spanish-First Terminology
OFICIAL: for all confirmed information (e.g., result announcements, game postponements).
En Desarrollo: used when new information is still coming in (e.g., a developing story).
Confirmado: used when something is verified and final.
Actualizado: to indicate new information has replaced outdated content (e.g., corrected score).
Do’s and Don’ts for Tone
Do:
Use clear, factual language.
Maintain a neutral, calm tone, especially when reporting uncertainty.
Always label uncertain or developing information with appropriate terms.
Don’t:
Use sensational language or clickbait.
Speculate on outcomes before information is verified.
Ignore the importance of labeling when information is unclear.
Consistent Formatting
Caps Usage:
Use ALL CAPS for key updates like OFICIAL or FINAL.
Capitalize game results, but avoid ALL CAPS for every sentence.
Punctuation:
Use em dashes for breaks or to highlight the next step (e.g., “Confirmado: El evento se ha reprogramado – próximo partido será el [fecha]”).
Avoid excessive use of exclamation marks, emojis, or other elements that could seem overly dramatic.
Emojis:
Use 🚨 for urgent updates or breaking news.
✅ for confirmed updates.
3) Approved Phrasing Library
Common Update Types
Final Score Update

Spanish: "Final: [Equipo A] [3]–[2] [Equipo B]. Gracias por seguirnos."
English: "Final: [Team A] [3]–[2] [Team B]. Thanks for following."
Postponement Announcement

Spanish: "OFICIAL: El partido de [Equipo A] vs [Equipo B] ha sido pospuesto debido a [razón]. Nueva fecha será anunciada pronto."
English: "OFFICIAL: The match between [Team A] vs [Team B] has been postponed due to [reason]. New date will be announced soon."
Developing / Unverified Information

Spanish: "En desarrollo: Se están reportando rumores de [evento], pero no confirmado por fuentes oficiales. Última actualización: [hora]."
English: "Developing: Reports indicate [event], but not confirmed by official sources. Last update: [time]."
Correction/Updated Information

Spanish: "Corrección: El marcador que publicamos anteriormente era incorrecto. Confirmado ahora: [marcador correcto]."
English: "Correction: The score we posted earlier was incorrect. Confirmed now: [correct score]."
4) Testing Plan
3 Paper Scenarios to Validate:
“Postponement announcement”: Ensure the phrasing is clear, respectful, and professional.
“Correction to a previously posted result”: Verify that the correction language is transparent and informative.
“Delayed update message”: Ensure tone is neutral, no unnecessary urgency, and users know when to expect a next update.
